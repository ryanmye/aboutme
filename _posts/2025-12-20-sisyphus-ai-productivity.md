Last semester I built **Sisyphus** with a team for a course project—a productivity app that holds you accountable by verifying tasks with AI. We placed **Top 5 out of 40+ teams**.

## The Idea

Set a task and a time limit. When the timer ends, you submit a photo as proof. An LLM (we used Claude) checks whether the photo actually shows the completed task. No photo or failed verification = the commitment is broken. Simple, but effective for forcing follow-through.

## Tech Stack

- **Backend:** Node.js, Express
- **AI:** Claude API for image + text understanding
- **Frontend:** Basic HTML/CSS/JS (we focused on the core loop)

## What I Learned

1. **Prompting matters** — Getting the LLM to reliably distinguish "did homework" vs "took a photo of a textbook" required careful prompt design.
2. **User flow > features** — We cut scope to nail the core experience. A working timer + verification loop beat a half-baked dashboard.
3. **Course constraints** — Limited time and team size meant we shipped something small that worked rather than something big that didn't.
